## Methodology

A discovery session spread was conducted between representatives from TM Global and the Nigeria YEIB Investment Fund Investment Fund team (consisting of NSIA, DBN & AfDB).

The discovery was broken into two key sessions to understand:
* The Business
* The Brand

The objectives of these two sessions were:

* **Session 1:** An analysis of the business with the YEIB Investment Fund stewards to understand the business and industry. The focus was on brand positioning, differentiation and audience clarification to gain a better understanding of YEIB Investment Fund's Ecosystem within the context of its market and offerings.
* **Session 2:** Defining specific indicators to shape market perception and verbal positioning of the YEIB Investment Fund brand to determine brand perception, reception and retention. The focus was on terminologies that guide behaviour, tone and voice of the brand to shape the brand narrative and influence communication, tenets that are fundamental to the brand's core.

The ultimate goal of the session was to inform a creative strategy crafted to help YEIB Investment Fund position in the market and inform its communications.

This document distils the sessions into two parts that define how YEIB Investment Fund can maximise its position in the market.

## Our Findings

"Youth are not a risk to be managed - they are an asset class waiting to be unlocked."  
*— AfDB Director, Ahmed Attout*

* Nigeria's population is projected to grow from 262.4 million to 312.7 million between 2030 and 2040, then from 312.7 million to 401.3 million between 2040 and 2050. Over 60% of these 400 million people will be under the age of 35.
* This means the future of the country, its tax base, its stability, its economic output, its global standing rests almost entirely on what happens to young Nigerians in the next two decades.
* Every year, about 3.5 million Nigerians enter the labour force, but the formal sector creates less than 80,000 jobs.
* This leaves a large and growing employment gap that the formal economy cannot absorb on its own.
* As a result, Nigeria needs more young people to build businesses, not just seek jobs, and YEIB Investment Fund matters because it can help arm the youth and women with the financial and non-financial support that they need to grow.

* For the first time in years, Nigeria's economic conditions are creating room for businesses to grow.
* Inflation which peaked at 33.24% in 2024 declined to 21.88% by mid-2025, its lowest in over two years.
* Consumer spending is recovering, and currency reforms are beginning to hold.
* However, this window will only benefit businesses with access to capital and support to grow through it.
* Even when capital exists, many youth and women entrepreneurs in Nigeria still cannot access it due to lack of awareness.
* Others have been burned by failed programmes before and no longer trust that anything is different.
* And many do not yet have the financial literacy to apply.
* For YEIB Investment Fund to truly succeed and reach the people it was built for, we must create awareness deliberately, provide financial literacy support, and make the application process simple, clear, and easy to navigate.
* DFIs disburse money annually through enterprises to enable businesses across different sectors grow.
* However, by the time capital flows from a DFI through a commercial bank to an SME, interest rates have been marked up because the banks consider their margins, risk premiums, and collateral requirements.
* The entrepreneur at the end of the chain receives none of the original advantage, indicating the need for YEIB to de-risk the banks more effectively or create pathways that allow capital to reach SMEs more directly.
* Every institution that has successfully deployed capital at scale from the CDFI Fund to BPI France, etc., started with a deliberately small group.
* The evidence from SME financing programmes consistently shows that funding a fixed cohort of businesses, paired with mentorship and non-financial support, produces better outcomes than broad deployment.
* For YEIB, a cohort approach will enable us learn what works in the Nigerian context, iterate before scaling, and generate the proof we need to build the confidence of investors.

## Brand Differentiation

An ecosystem for MSME's that partners with you throughout the life cycle of your business. We provide full business cycle support to youth and women-led businesses. We meet businesses wherever they are in their journey and provide the right financial and non-financial support needed to help them grow, mature, and become sustainable.

We deliver this through three core differentiators:

### 1. Our Capital Structure
Most institutions provide one type of funding. YEIB Investment Fund provides a structured capital model designed to support youth- and women-led businesses at different stages while reducing the barriers that have excluded them.
* **Equity Investments:** YEIB Investment Fund provides growth capital directly to businesses and indirectly through Venture Capital (VC) and Private Equity (PE) funds that already have strong investment expertise. This approach strengthens the broader investment ecosystem while increasing the amount of capital available to youth- and women-led enterprises.
* **Credit Guarantees:** Many young businesses struggle to access loans because banks view them as too risky or lacking sufficient collateral. We absorb this risk through our Credit Guarantee Fund, which offers first loss coverage up to 75%. By absorbing a portion of potential losses, we give financial institutions the confidence to lend to businesses they would otherwise overlook.
* **Ecosystem Development Funding:** YEIB Investment Fund recognises that capital alone does not build successful businesses. 10% of our funding is dedicated to strengthening the ecosystem that supports entrepreneurs, including technical assistance, business advisory, investment readiness programmes, research, and institutional support. This ensures businesses are not only funded, but also equipped to succeed.

### 2. Growth Readiness
Through our network of Enterprise Support Organisations (ESOs) and Business Development Service (BDS) providers, YEIB Investment Fund ensures that every business we support has access to the technical assistance, capacity building, and mentorship it needs to grow, mature, and become sustainable. Our partnerships ensures that the businesses become stronger, more resilient, and investment-ready over time.

### 3. Future Focused
YEIB Investment Fund is built on the belief that opportunity should not be determined by connection, privilege, or geography. ## Brand Outlook in the Future (5 – 10 Years)

Over the next 5-10 years, YEIB Investment Fund is committed to becoming the institution that permanently changes what is possible for young and women-led businesses in Nigeria. We aim to achieve these impacts:

* **1. Institutional Leadership:** Establish YEIB Investment Fund as the most trusted and credible DFI Fund in Nigeria's landscape such that institutional investors, commercial banks, young entrepreneurs, and the general public recognise YEIB Investment Fund as an institution that does what it says, publishes what it finds, and is governed independent of political interference.
* **2. Sustain Businesses:** Sustain jobs and businesses within the next five years with beneficiaries being entrepreneurs in different sectors across the nation.
* **3. Shift Risk Appetite:** Prove that youth and women-led businesses in Nigeria are a commercially investable asset class through evidence of the thriving business that have been established through our investment fund. Through this we shift the risk appetite of more tier-one and tier-two commercial banks toward youth and women-led MSME lending.
* **4. Mobilise Capital:** Mobilise at least \$500 million in private and institutional co-investment capital for the youth and women MSME market.

## Strategic Steps to Achieve This Success

* **1. Deploy Capital in Cohorts:** We deploy capital into structured cohorts of youth and women-led businesses, track every business quarterly, and publish a comprehensive audited performance report at the end of each cycle. This approach enables us test what works, sharpen the institution's model, and builds the evidence base we need to expand our impact.

* **3. Convene Events:** We will host events that bring key stakeholders into the same room. These forums will gather institutional investors, commercial banks, government officials, ecosystem partners, and entrepreneurs to share progress, build relationships, and open new deal opportunities for the startups and young entrepreneurs.
* **4. Beneficiary Storytelling:** From the first cohort, we document and deploy the stories of our beneficiaries - at investor forums, on community radio, and across social media. These stories put a human face on YEIB Investment Fund's impact, build trust with communities who have been let down before, and show the world that the youth and women can anchor businesses that grow remarkably, when they are provided with the right support.

## One Thing

YEIB Investment Fund's one thing is **"Ally"**

We are the institutional ally that the youth and women-led MSMEs need to grow sustainably. We actively stand beside them to not only provide capital, but also the ecosystem with partnerships and support. Here, no entrepreneur walks through our door and feels like a risk. They feel like the solution. We help them build credibility, access the right capital, and grow strong enough to create jobs, attract investment, and contribute meaningfully to Nigeria's future.

## Brand Purpose

To create a bankable future for Nigerian youth and women.

MSMEs are the backbone of Nigeria's economy, contributing 48% of GDP and generating 84% of employment. Behind most of these businesses are young Nigerians and women entrepreneurs who have the ideas, the drive, and the determination to build. When they are de-risked and given the capital, access, and ecosystem they need to grow, they do not just build businesses, they build lives worth staying for. They invest back into the communities around them, create jobs for other women and young Nigerians, while becoming the kind of thriving entrepreneurs that make Nigeria increasingly attractive to foreign investors. We are committed to giving every growth-minded woman and youth the support they need to build, sustain, and own their future here.
